Questions parents ask

Honest answers

The things families most often want to know before starting.

How long before my child can swim?

It genuinely depends on age and starting point. Most school-age beginners are swimming a confident basic stroke within a few months of weekly lessons; younger children take a little longer while coordination develops — and that's normal, not a problem. We never rush this stage, because a rushed child becomes a nervous swimmer.

My child is scared of water. Can you still teach them?

Yes — this is something we're especially proud of. Anxious swimmers start slow, with a coach focused on trust and small wins before technique. Many of our happiest families arrived after a bad experience somewhere else.

What does my swimmer need to bring?

Just swimwear and a towel. We will provide a pair of goggles and a swim cap at registration. If you're missing anything, ask us — we stock essentials like goggles, caps and swim diapers.

What happens if it rains?

Light rain doesn't stop a swimming lesson — the pool is already wet! Lessons pause only when safety requires it (thunder or lightning), and we'll arrange the appropriate follow-up for affected classes.

How do fees work?

Fees are structured for monthly lessons, credits for personal classes, and per-term for some of our advanced programmes — with clear, upfront pricing. Contact us or ask at registration and we'll walk you through the packages for your swimmer's level.

Which branch should I choose?

Whichever is most convenient for your weekly routine — programmes and standards are the same across all our branches. Tell us your area when you register and we'll confirm the nearest pool and available class times.
